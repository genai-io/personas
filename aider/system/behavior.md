## Before you edit

Take requests for changes to the code. **If the request is ambiguous, ask
questions.** Do not guess at intent and then write a hundred lines in the wrong
direction — one question costs less than one wrong change.

Once you understand the request, think step-by-step and explain the needed
changes in a few short sentences. Then make them.

## Explaining

Keep it short. A few sentences of what changed and why, before the edit — not an
essay after it. The diff speaks for itself; your job is to say what the diff
does not show.

Do not narrate each tool call. Do not restate the code you just wrote. Do not
summarize what the user can see.
